Fast & Easy Installation

ProAqCT is vendor agnostic Dose Monitoring system compatible with all CT, Fluoroscopy, PET, and nuclear medicine vendors.

One of the many ways ProAqCT offers an affordable dose management solution is with a light IT footprint. Because ProAqCT runs in an OS-level virtual container, it can be remotely deployed behind the network firewall on a virtual server or a physical machine.

We also offer deployment on a HIPPA compliant AWS cloud service. The cloud service eliminates the need for costly server hardware on-site as well as organizational IT resources. AWS expertly supports all server maintenance to relieve your organization's IT burden.

Our team of IT specialists provide all necessary technical resources and work with your organization to deliver fast and expert end-to-end installation.

Optimial Image Quality at the Lowest Dose!

Track & Optimize Scanner Image Quality with Automated Image Quality Monitoring

ProAqCT ensures optimal imaging at the lowest doses by automatically monitoring clinical image quality in addition to patient dose. Using machine learning, ProAqCT performs objective image quality measurements on patient scans and trends image quality vs dose. This allows users to benchmark image quality metrics vs. dose across different scanners per protocol, standardize protocols, and maximize quality in a patient-specific manner.

Objective Image quality metrics, such as average noise, can be compared across scanner protocols. Our Image Quality analytics are designed to provide insights into protocol and scanner performance that lead to more optimal and standard imaging practices.

Automated Protocol Mapping

ProAqCT uses machine learning to precisely map protocols to Radlex or any other lexicon. A mapping score, indicating the accuracy of the mapping selection, is provided for each mapped exam. When mapping accuracy falls below 90%, users receive alert notifications so that the mapping can be verified. This powerful mapping technique improves dose data analysis and prevents alert fatigue by reducing the number of false positive alerts.

Simplified Patient Dose Review

ProAqCT makes it easy to review patient doses. A summary of dose indices that exceed alert thresholds is clearly provided with each exam. The patient AP and Lateral dimensions are automatically determined from axial slice data to give accurate measurements of the patient’s effective diameter and water equivalent diameter (WED). Patient scan length and iso-centering data are also provided to facilitate the review of patient dose.

Interactive 3D Peak Skin Dose

ProAqCT delivers the most accurate patient peak skin dose calculation on the market. Using the full structured dose report, 3-D Peak Skin Dose maps are calculated for cath and IR procedures from any manufacture. ProAqCT achieves high accuracy peak skin dose maps by factoring system calibration factors, backscatter contribution, table attenuation, and all patient and beam geometry. The interactive UI allows users to rotate the antropromorphic phantom 360 degrees to review all areas of exposure. Automated Medical Physics Dose Evaluation reports that comply with the AMA 2021 publication of CPT 76145 are produced with one-click.

Monitor Nuclear Medicine Doses, Including Therapies, PET and SPECT!

ProAqCT applies the Medical Internal Radiation (MIRD) formalism to calculate organ and effective dose estimates for nuclear medicine and PET exams. The MIRD method defines absorbed dose as the product of time-integrated activity in a source organ, which represents the biokinetics of a radiopharmaceutical, and organ dose conversion factors called S values. To get the most accurate internal dosimetry estimates, ProAqCT implements the NCI library of S values and biokinetic data for more than 300 clinically relevant radiopharmaceuticals. This extensive library of S values is a function of 300 radionuclides, 55 target regions, 68 source regions, and 24 phantoms.

Accurate & Reproducible Peak Skin Dose Calculation

  • Because discrete anthropomorphic phantoms do not always correspond to the patient's body habitus, users can also calculate PSD using our cylindrical phantoms.
  • The major and minor axes, as well as the length of the cylinder, can be adjusted to approximate any patient’s body habitus.
  • The cylinder phantoms provide an efficient way to estimate the patient’s entrance point and can improve the accuracy of the PSD calculation.
  • Beam geometery and correction factors such as table pad thickness, calibration factors for air kerma at reference point, attenuation coefficients, backscatter factors, and patient offset from isocenter can be adjusted by the user.
